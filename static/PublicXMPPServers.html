PublicXMPPServers

Updated

XMPP (earlier named Jabber) is a federated protocol, meaning that you can sign up at any public server and talk to users on all other public servers. The visual difference is your account name—if you sign up with creep.im, the account name you enter into your chat client will be username@creep.im.

Facebook actually uses XMPP for its chat feature. You can log into Facebook Chat using your username@facebook.com credentials with any XMPP client. Unfortunately their service is not public, so you can only chat with other Facebook users.

There are many public XMPP servers for you to choose from, and these are only a few (you can often register on their website, and otherwise directly in your chat client):

  • jabber.org
    The oldest, original public server.
  • jabber.se
    An old and very fast Swedish server.
  • creep.im
    Short and sweet, with humor.
  • rows.io
    An even shorter, Canadian server.
  • unstable.nl
    A green Dutch server, also sporting humor.

More public servers are listed at xmpp.net.
